      Load your unit according to need.

      Load your unit according to need. If you’re storing items you may need access to later, such as holiday decorations or lawn equipment, make sure you pack them near the front. When loading your self storage unit, pack the items you know you’ll need the least access to in the back and continue forward, with the most frequently used items near the door.

      Consider the facility’s safety features.

      Check to see if there is adequate lighting around the building and that access to the self storage facility is controlled either by a gate or code lock. Ask about access times and how the facility grants access to the units, as well as surveillance cameras and monitoring.

      Label EVERYTHING.

      Label EVERYTHING. Specific labels will help make finding that ONE item later a breeze, especially if you provide a short inventory on the box. Each "Kitchen" box should also include a short list of contents like "pots and pans" "dish towels, aprons, trays" so you don’t waste time opening the wrong box.

      Load your unit properly to avoid theft and hazards.

      Don’t put easily carried, valuable items like TVs or stereos near the front; hide them behind other boxes to deter theft. When stacking boxes, start with heavier, larger boxes on the bottom and put smaller or fragile item boxes on top.

      Clean it before you store it.

      To avoid mold and mildew damage to your stored items, especially household appliances, make sure they are clean and dry before being placed in your self storage unit. Run appliances like dishwashers on an empty cycle to remove residue and leave the doors to refrigerators slightly open to allow airflow.

      Consider storage insurance.

      First find out if your other insurance, such as car insurance, already protects your stored items. Ask your storage facility if they require insurance, and if they have preferred insurance companies to recommend. Consider private storage insurance if you are storing very valuable or sentimentally important items.

      Quantify your storage load.

      Take stock of how big the items are that you need to store when choosing both the size of your self storage unit and a transportation method. Always estimate a bit over in the size unit you’ll need, as it’s best to have too much space than too little. Also consider if you’ll need a moving truck to get your stored items from the storage facility to home, or if a few friends and cars can do ...

      Measure large items before moving.

      If you have a large or awkward item like a couch or art sculpture, be sure to measure it first. Consider the building entrances and if you’ll be able to fit large items through the doors. To avoid having an item not fit when it comes time to load your unit, double check the dimensions first.

      Don't store it if you don't need it.

      As you pack boxes up for storage, go through your items and consider if they’re necessary to store. You may find that many items you’re holding on to are better donated to charity or sold at a yard sale. Consider your self storage to be an opportunity for some spring cleaning!

      Read your self storage agreement.

      Don’t get caught off guard by hidden administrative fees, penalties, and other clauses that may exist in your agreement. Take the time to go over each part of your contract before signing so you’re aware of all of the facility policies and guidelines.

      Pick your boxes carefully.

      Try to use similarly sized boxes to help when stacking them later in your self storage unit. Avoid flimsy boxes as they could break when loaded with heavier items. When reusing old boxes make sure they’re clean and dry to avoid risking mildew and mold growth.

      Choose the right location.

      If you’re using self storage as a temporary solution for moving from home to home, consider the location of your storage facility. Decide if it will be more convenient to have your storage unit near your old house or your new one. You should also compare prices in the two areas as well; depending on the areas there may be substantial differences in price.

      Get help for the heavy stuff.

      Don’t tackle the heavy items alone, grab a few friends and load as a team. Take advantage of any free loading equipment that your storage facility provides like carts or dollies and save the stress on your back. If you’re lifting a lot of heavy items, you may want to look into lift assist straps or a lumbar support "back support belt".

      Safe on packing material.

      If you have old sheets, towels or other non-fragile linens that you don’t mind getting a little wrinkled use them as cushioning around odd-shaped items in boxes. The box will protect the cloth from dirt, and you’ll save money on packing materials.

      Always ask about discounts.

      Many storage facilities provide discounts to students and military personnel. Some may offer members of AAA or AARP a discount, as well as members of local organizations. Many self storage facilities also offer discounted rates if you pay for your storage up front rather than month-to-month.
